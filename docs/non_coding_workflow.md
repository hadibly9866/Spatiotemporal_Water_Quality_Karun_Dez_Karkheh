# Non-Coding Workflow Notes

This document outlines the parts of the research workflow that were performed manually or using non-scripted tools, supplementing the computational analysis.

## 1. Diagrammatic Analysis

### 1.1. Piper Diagram Analysis
- Visual interpretation of the Piper diagram to classify water types based on major ion composition.
- Identification of dominant hydrogeochemical facie.
- Notes on patterns observed and their relation to geological formations or anthropogenic influences.

### 1.2. Stiff Diagram Analysis
- Use of Stiff diagrams for detailed representation of ionic composition at specific stations.
- Comparison of ionic signatures across different locations or time periods.

### 1.3. Gibbs Diagram Interpretation
- Analysis of Gibbs diagrams to infer the primary mechanisms controlling water chemistry (rock-water interaction, evaporation, precipitation).
- Correlation of Gibbs diagram results with geological context and other analytical findings.

## 2. Introduction and Conclusion Synthesis

- **Introduction:** Narrative synthesis of background information, problem statement, and study objectives.
- **Discussion:** Interpretation of results in the context of existing literature, discussing implications, limitations, and potential sources of error not covered by code.
- **Conclusion:** Summary of key findings and recommendations for future research or management.

## 3. Literature Review and Conceptualization

- Integration of relevant scientific literature to support the interpretation of findings.
- Conceptual model development based on integrated analysis of hydrochemistry, remote sensing, and spatial patterns.
